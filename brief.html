Situation

Event Summary

On the evening of April 25 into the early hours of April 26, 2026, a severe weather outbreak impacted a wide corridor of North Texas from Archer County through the DFW Metroplex and into Grayson/Fannin Counties. The event produced confirmed tornadoes, widespread destructive hail up to 4.00", and straight-line winds up to 89 mph. Two fatalities, six injuries, and three disaster declarations have been confirmed.

Storm Chase Roofers Active in Grayson County
Christian Galvan (local roofer) posted on Facebook April 26: out-of-town roofers are already knocking doors in Whitewright, Trenton, Leonard, Tom Bean, and Paris. "Telling people whatever they need to hear just to get a signature." Matt — expect competition on the ground at Tom Bean.
Deductible Sticker Shock = PA Value Proposition
The 2% wind/hail deductible standard is hitting DFW homeowners hard. One Cockrell Hill resident documented a $13,120 deductible (implies ~$656K dwelling coverage). Homeowners need PA representation to ensure the full claim amount exceeds the deductible meaningfully.

Field Reference

Hail Impact Identification Guide

Reference images showing the types of hail damage to look for during field inspections. Click any image to enlarge.

Standing Seam Metal Roof
Circular dents and cracks at seam from hail impacts. Look for dimpled depressions along flat panels and cracking at seam joints.
Bob Behrends Roofing
Standing seam denting — panel surface sinks in due to weight and force of hailstone impact. Visible under raking light.
Waddle Exteriors
Asphalt Shingle Damage
Multiple dark hail impact marks showing granule displacement. Look for circular dark spots where granules are knocked away exposing the asphalt mat.
Bob Behrends Roofing
1.5" hail damage to nearly-new asphalt shingles — round impact areas where granules have been removed exposing fresh black asphalt substrate.
InspectAPedia
Close-up of scouring and granule loss. Note the exposed mat where protective granule coating has been stripped by impact.
InspectAPedia
HVAC Condenser Units
Condenser coil fins — hail-bent fins reduce airflow. Look for flattened, bent, and crushed aluminum fins across the coil array.
Warren Forensics
HVAC condenser showing damage to external fins and cabinet — a commonly overlooked claimable component during hail inspections.
InspectAPedia
Siding — Vinyl & Aluminum
Aluminum siding with hail indentations — wetting the surface makes impressions easier to see. Look for circular dimples peppered across panel faces.
InterNACHI
Spatter marks where hail impact removed surface oxidation. These marks re-oxidize and fade over time — document early.
InterNACHI
Windows & Screens
Window screen showing streaking and puncture damage from hail. Screens are often the first indicator of hail direction and severity.
InterNACHI
Window glass broken by large hail impact — document glass breakage along with frame and sill pitting for the full window claim.
All American Window & Screen
Soft Metals — Gutters, Downspouts, Flashing
Gutter bottom with indentations from hailstones falling directly into the channel. Soft aluminum shows clear circular dent pattern.
InterNACHI
Gutters and downspouts with hail dents — indentations are typically smaller than the diameter of the hail that caused them.
InterNACHI
Dented metal flashing — soft metal trim components clearly show impact force that adjacent shingles also absorbed.
InspectAPedia
Vehicle Damage (Hail Size Reference)
Vehicle with hail dents across body panels — circular depressions on hood and roof indicate hail size and density during the event.
Wikimedia Commons
Peppered dent pattern across flat body panels — flat horizontal surfaces (hood, roof) take the heaviest impacts. Use as proxy for stone size.
