Situation

Event Summary

On the evening of April 25 into the early hours of April 26, 2026, a severe weather outbreak impacted a wide corridor of North Texas from Archer County through the DFW Metroplex and into Grayson/Fannin Counties. The event produced confirmed tornadoes, widespread destructive hail up to 4.00", and straight-line winds up to 89 mph. Two fatalities, six injuries, and three disaster declarations have been confirmed.

Situational Awareness

Field Intelligence

Attorneys Already Advertising
John Wood / dragonslayerlawyers.com is actively soliciting on the Facebook Crisis Response page for this event. Time-sensitive to reach claimants before attorney retention locks us out.
Storm Chase Roofers Active in Grayson County
Christian Galvan (local roofer) posted on Facebook April 26: out-of-town roofers are already knocking doors in Whitewright, Trenton, Leonard, Tom Bean, and Paris. "Telling people whatever they need to hear just to get a signature." Matt — expect competition on the ground at Tom Bean.
Deductible Sticker Shock = PA Value Proposition
The 2% wind/hail deductible standard is hitting DFW homeowners hard. One Cockrell Hill resident documented a $13,120 deductible (implies ~$656K dwelling coverage). Homeowners need PA representation to ensure the full claim amount exceeds the deductible meaningfully.
