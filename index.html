Overview

Event at a Glance

A powerful supercell thunderstorm complex produced very large hail, damaging straight-line winds, an EF-2 tornado, and flash flooding across North Texas from Saturday evening April 25 through early Sunday morning April 26, 2026. The primary supercell ignited in Wichita County at approximately 4:00 PM CDT and tracked southeast for nearly 12 continuous hours through the heart of the DFW Metroplex before dissipating around 3:30 AM CDT Sunday.

Deployment Strategy

Priority Deployment Targets — Commercial Focus

Ranked by commercial client opportunity, accounting for hail severity, population density, commercial corridor concentration, and claim complexity. Residential deployment potential noted where applicable.

1

Tarrant County — Azle / Lakeside / White Settlement / Fort Worth

3.00" confirmed hail • 70 mph ASOS wind • Flash flooding

Heaviest concentration of SPC hail reports in the entire event. 3.00" at Azle and Lakeside, 2.75" at White Settlement and NNW Fort Worth, 2.50" at White Settlement, 2.00" at Benbrook and Kennedale. Flash flooding in SW Fort Worth creates dual-peril claims with complex coverage questions (watch for ACC denials). The western Tarrant corridor contains significant commercial strip centers, warehousing, industrial parks, and multi-family housing. Fort Worth CBD and surrounding commercial districts sustained 60–70+ mph winds.

3.00" Hail 70 mph Wind Flash Flood Commercial Priority Residential
2

Dallas County — Cockrell Hill / SW Dallas / Grand Prairie

3.00" confirmed hail • 60 mph ASOS wind • Highest claim volume potential

3.00" confirmed baseball-size hail at Cockrell Hill / Dallas Baptist University area with photo documentation. 2.50" also confirmed at the same location. The southern Dallas County corridor (Cockrell Hill through Duncanville to Oak Leaf) contains dense commercial retail, auto dealerships, logistics facilities, and multi-family complexes. At 3.00", expect total-loss roofing on commercial flat and low-slope systems, HVAC damage, window and storefront damage, and significant vehicle fleet losses.

3.00" Hail 60 mph Wind Commercial Priority Residential
3

Parker County — Springtown / Aledo / Willow Park

89 mph measured wind • 2.50" hail • Structural collapse • 1 fatality

Highest measured straight-line wind in the event at 89 mph (Springtown). 2.50" hail confirmed at Aledo via photo. Manufactured homes toppled, vehicles overturned, structural collapses documented across the Springtown corridor. The Aledo–Willow Park corridor along I-20 contains commercial development, equestrian and agricultural operations, and high-value residential. Wind claims at 89 mph substantiate significant structural damage beyond what standard hail-only claims would support.

2.50" Hail 89 mph Wind Tornado Warning Commercial Priority Residential
4

Wise County — Runaway Bay / Bridgeport

EF-2 Tornado (135 mph) • 2.75" hail • Disaster declaration

Compound peril: EF-2 tornado with 135 mph peak winds confirmed at two structures plus 2.75" hail — both at Runaway Bay. Disaster declaration issued. 20–40 families displaced. Dozens of homes with major damage. Commercial opportunities are limited due to rural character but include marinas, small commercial on Lake Bridgeport, agricultural operations, and municipal properties. Residential deployment is strong here given the disaster declaration and displacement.

Commercial Claim Complexity
Commercial hail claims in this event will involve business interruption (BI) considerations for properties with interior water intrusion from compromised roofing, code upgrade requirements for roof systems that have reached end of useful life, and potential ACV-vs-RCV disputes on older commercial roofing. Multi-tenant retail and industrial properties may require coordination with multiple lessees for access and interior damage documentation.

Texas Insurance Code Chapter 542, Subchapter B — Prompt Payment of Claims Act. Penalties: 18% per annum interest + attorney's fees (§ 542.060); reduced to post-judgment rate + 5% for weather claims under Chapter 542A.

Key Watch Items

  • Weather catastrophe extension (§ 542.059): Given the scale of this event (23 counties, disaster declaration), TDI Commissioner may declare a weather-related catastrophe, extending all Ch. 542 deadlines by an additional 15 days. Monitor TDI bulletins.
  • Chapter 542A presuit notice: For weather-related property claims, a 61-day presuit notice is required before filing suit (§ 542A.003(a)).
  • 2% wind/hail deductible is the dominant DFW market standard (2026). On a $1M commercial property, that is a $20,000 deductible.
  • Anti-concurrent causation (ACC) — watch for denials where wind and flood overlap (especially Tarrant County SW Fort Worth).
  • Cosmetic damage exclusion endorsements — review policies for metal roof exclusions before scoping.
  • Appraisal clause — critical leverage when carrier estimates fall short of actual replacement cost.
  • Statute of limitations: 2 years from storm date (April 25, 2028).
